Conditional Sentences | 04

Learn The Subject

[1] Definition

Conditional sentences are statements discussing known factors or hypothetical situations and their consequences.Complete conditional sentences contain a conditional clause (often referred to as the if-clause) and the consequence.The conditional clause usually begins with if or unless and can usually come before or after the main clause.In general,conditional sentences used to explain about cause and effect of an event.

When can we use conditional sentences? You can use this sentence in situations like the following:

  • • Declare events that are not real,just what do you imagine
  • • Reveals events that may occur in the future
  • • Indicates events that are unlikely to occur
  • • Shows general truths or common habits
  • • Telling past events that didn't happen as expected, then regret occurred.

[1] Types | Conditonal Sentence

In general,there's four types of conditional sentences,every type has it's own purpose and rule on it's usage.However, in this lesson will discuss all the 4 types of conditional sentences so that your grammar knowledge is more complete. Besides grammar, you will also meet several tenses in English.Lets jump straight into it.

A.Conditional Sentence type 0

Conditional sentence type 0 is used to state the cause and effect of a fact that is generally known to be true. If thing A happens, it will cause thing B.

Formula:

if + subject + Verb 1, subject + Verb 1 + Complement

OR

Simple Present Tense + if + Simple Present Tense





Example:

  • • If you heat the butter,it melts.
  • • If the iceberg melt,polar bear lost their home.
  • •If you eath your lunch box,your mom feels happy.
  • •The ground gets wet if it rains.
  • •She comforts me if i feel weak.

B.Conditional Sentence type 1

Conditional sentence type 1 is used to express something that is very likely to happen in the future if we do something.

Formula:
if + Simple present tense, simple future tense

OR

Simple future Tense + if + Simple Present Tense





Example:

  • • If you you go now, you will arive there on time.
  • • my sister will buy me a new PS5 if i pass the exam.
  • •If if you take a picture of this statue, you will be arrested.
  • •You will get sick if you play in rain.
  • •If you win the prize,she will be happy.

C.Conditional Sentence type 2

Conditional sentence type 2 is used to express something that is unrealistic or very unlikely to happen. Therefore, this type is suitable for expressing our wishes.

Formula:

if + Simple present past, simple present conditional

OR

Present Conditional + if + Simple Past





Example:

  • •If i lived in Tokyo,i would go to the concert.
  • • i would play PS5 everyday if i owned one.
  • •If she worked hard,she would be the best employee.
  • •If i was you,i would marry her since last year.

D.Conditional Sentence type 3

Conditional sentence type 3 is used to convey wish about events that have occurred in the past. Because the reality has already happened and cannot be changed anymore, we can only express what we want happen if the incident goes differently from reality.

Formula:


if + past perfect, perfect conditional

OR

Perfect conditional + if + past perfect





Example:

  • •If I had left my house earlier, I wouldn’t have missed the train.
  • • If she had called me yesterday, I would have been happy.
  • •If eight years ago i had more money, i would have bought a yacht.
  • •If he had more time, she wouldn't have to leave him.
