So,Such | 01

Learn The Subject

[1] Definition | So,Such

In English, the word 'So' is an adverb, while 'such' is a determiner. The equation of these two words means very. The function of both is to strengthen the meaning of adjectives, adverbs, or nouns.

[1] Description | So,Such

To understand the material about so that and such that, consider the following differences! The difference lies only in the placement of the Part of Speech. After the word 'such' it is followed by a noun phrase, while after the word 'so' it is followed by an adjective.The phrase so that expresses cause and effect sentences.

A.So ... That

So ... That usually used to express cause and effect.

Formula:

... + so + (Adjective/Adverb) + that + ...

Example:

  • + Cause: It was too windy.
  • - Effect: We couldn't go sailing.
  • > Result: It was so windy that we couldn't go sailing. (windy - adjective)

  • + Cause: His PC Build was wonderfully designed.
  • - Effect: I couldn't take my eyes off it.
  • > Result: His PC build was so wonderfully designed that i couldn't take my eyes off it.

Note: So ... that can be used with quntifiers(many,much,few,little,etc.) if there's noun. (Example: She has so few free time that makes me rarely meet her.)



B.Such ... That

Alike with 'So ... that','Such ... that' used to express cause and effect.

Formula:

... + such + (a/an) + Adjective + noun + that* + ...

*That is optional,does not change the meaning of a sentence.



Example:

  • • His persia is such a cute cat that we always miss her.
  • • Jacob is such a smart boy that she always win a competition.
  • • That such an amazing engine that amazed everybody.
